1.2 Differential Calculus

1.2.1 Ordinary Derivatives

1.2.2 Gradient

$$\nabla r = \frac{\partial r}{\partial x}\hat{x}+\frac{\partial r}{\partial y}\hat{y}+\frac{\partial r}{\partial z}\hat{z}$$

1.2.3 The Del Operator

$$\nabla = \hat{x}\frac{\partial}{\partial x}+\hat{y}\frac{\partial}{\partial y}+\hat{z}\frac{\partial}{\partial z}$$ The gradient is nabla scalar-product.

1.2.4 The Divergence

Divergence is nabla dot-product.

1.2.5 The Curl

Curl is nabla cross-product.

1.2.6 Product Rules

The generalized derivatives above satisfy many of the familiar derivative rules. Some which are not completely familiar are:
Gradient of dot $$\nabla(\vec{A}\cdot\vec{B}) = \vec{A}\times (\nabla \times \vec{B}) + \vec{B}\times (\nabla\times \vec{A}) + $$ $$(\vec{A}\cdot\nabla)\vec{B}+(\vec{B}\cdot\nabla)\vec{A}$$ Div of function $$\nabla \cdot(f\vec{A}) = f(\nabla\cdot\vec{A})+\vec{A}\cdot(\nabla f)$$ Div of cross $$\vec{B}\cdot(\nabla\times \vec{A})-\vec{A}\cdot(\nabla\times\vec{B})$$ Curl of function $$\nabla\times(f\vec{A})=f(\nabla\times\vec{A})-\vec{A}\times(\nabla f)$$ Curl of cross $$\nabla\times\vec{A}\times\vec{B} = (\vec{B}\cdot\nabla)\vec{A}-(\vec{A}\cdot\nabla)\vec{B}+\vec{A}(\nabla\cdot\vec{B}-\vec{B}(\nabla\cdot\vec{A}))$$

1.2.7 Second Derivatives

The Laplacian is the div of grad. It is also sometimes defined as the negative second term in $$\nabla\times(\nabla\times \vec{v}) = \nabla(\nabla\cdot\vec{v})-\nabla^2\vec{v}$$

The curl of a grad, and the div of a curl, are always 0.
